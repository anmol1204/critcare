AKI Staging (KDIGO)

StageSerum CreatinineUrine Output
Stage 1× 1.5–1.9 baseline OR rise ≥ 0.3 mg/dL within 48h< 0.5 ml/kg/h for 6–12h
Stage 2× 2.0–2.9 baseline< 0.5 ml/kg/h for ≥ 12h
Stage 3× 3.0 baseline OR Cr ≥ 4 mg/dL OR initiation of RRT< 0.3 ml/kg/h for ≥ 24h OR anuria ≥ 12h

Use whichever criterion (creatinine OR urine output) gives the higher stage. Baseline creatinine = lowest within 3 months, or estimate from MDRD using normal eGFR of 75 if unknown.

Causes & Work-up

Pre-renal

Hypovolaemia, cardiac failure, sepsis-induced renal vasodilatation, hepatorenal syndrome. FeNa <1%, urine Na <20, urine osmolality >500. Responds to volume or haemodynamic correction.

Intrinsic renal

  • ATN (most common in ICU): prolonged ischaemia, nephrotoxins (aminoglycosides, contrast, NSAIDs, vancomycin). Urine: muddy brown casts, FeNa >2%
  • AIN: drug-induced (beta-lactams, PPIs, NSAIDs) — eosinophiluria, WBC casts
  • GN: haematuria, RBC casts, proteinuria
  • TMA: TTP/HUS — schistocytes, thrombocytopenia, elevated LDH

Post-renal (obstruction)

Catheter blockage (first thing to rule out!), BPH, pelvic mass, bilateral ureteric obstruction. Ultrasound to check hydronephrosis.

Nephrotoxic drugs to withhold/adjust in AKI

Stop these in AKI
  • NSAIDs, COX-2 inhibitors
  • ACE inhibitors / ARBs (unless renal protective indication)
  • Aminoglycosides (switch to alternative if possible)
  • IV contrast (use iso-osmolar contrast, pre-hydrate)
  • Metformin (lactic acidosis risk)
  • Spironolactone (hyperkalaemia risk)

Conservative Management

  • Haemodynamic optimisation: maintain MAP ≥ 65 mmHg; correct hypovolaemia but avoid fluid overload (>10% weight gain worsens outcome)
  • Diuretics: furosemide to maintain urine output and manage volume — does NOT prevent AKI or improve renal recovery, but can avoid volume overload
  • Avoid nephrotoxins (see above)
  • Tight glycaemic control 140–180 mg/dL
  • Nutritional support — don't restrict protein in AKI; normal requirements (0.8–1.0 g/kg/day), higher if on CRRT (1.5–2 g/kg/day due to amino acid losses)
  • Prevent and treat hyperkalaemia (diet, calcium resonium, calcium gluconate for ECG changes)

Indications for RRT

Absolute indications (AEIOU) — start immediately
  • Acidosis — metabolic acidosis pH < 7.1 refractory to medical treatment
  • Electrolytes — hyperkalaemia K+ > 6.5 mEq/L with ECG changes or refractory to medical management
  • Ingestion — dialysable toxins (methanol, ethylene glycol, lithium, salicylates, metformin)
  • Overload — pulmonary oedema refractory to diuretics
  • Uraemia — urea >150–200 mg/dL, uraemic encephalopathy, uraemic pericarditis, uraemic bleeding

Timing of RRT

STARRT-AKI trial (2020): accelerated vs standard strategy — no difference in 90-day mortality. Avoid prophylactic RRT for AKI that may recover. Wait for absolute indication or AKI Stage 3 with clinical deterioration. 1A

CRRT Prescription

Modality: CVVHDF (preferred)

Continuous venovenous haemodiafiltration — combines convection (haemofiltration) and diffusion (haemodialysis). Best for haemodynamically unstable patients.

ParameterSettingNotes
Blood flow rate150–250 ml/minHigher if anticoagulation issues
Effluent dose20–25 ml/kg/hrKDIGO recommends ≥20; 25 prescribed to deliver 20 net (filter losses)
Dialysate flow50% of effluentFor CVVHDF; adjust per machine
UltrafiltrationPer fluid balance goalNet negative or neutral; set hourly target
Replacement fluidPre-dilution preferredReduces clotting, slightly lower clearance
AccessNon-dominant femoral or RIJJugular preferred; avoid subclavian (stenosis)

When to switch to intermittent HD

When patient is haemodynamically stable, fluid balance is controlled, and GFR remains low. IHD offers more rapid toxin/electrolyte clearance when needed.

Anticoagulation in CRRT

MethodUse whenProtocolMonitoring
Regional citrate (RCA)First choice — all patientsCitrate pre-filter → calcium replacement post-filterPost-filter iCa 0.25–0.35, systemic iCa 1.0–1.35
Unfractionated heparinNo citrate availability; HIT ruled out500–1000 IU/hr pre-filteraPTT 45–60s
No anticoagulationActive bleeding, high haemorrhage riskFlush circuit with saline q30–60minMonitor filter pressure, circuit life
Citrate accumulation

Suspect if: total Ca/ionised Ca ratio > 2.5, metabolic alkalosis, or liver failure (liver metabolises citrate). Reduce citrate dose or switch to heparin if citrate accumulation occurs.
