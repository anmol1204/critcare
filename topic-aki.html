How AKI Works — The Pathophysiology

The Kidney's Vulnerability

The kidney receives 20–25% of cardiac output but consumes only 8% of O₂. This combination makes the renal medulla exquisitely sensitive to ischaemia — the medullary thick ascending limb (mTAL) has the highest O₂ consumption in the body and the lowest O₂ delivery. Any reduction in renal blood flow rapidly causes medullary hypoxia.

Mechanisms of AKI

  • Haemodynamic (pre-renal → ATN): ↓ renal perfusion pressure → afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction (autoregulation) → ↓ GFR. Prolonged → ischaemia → tubular cell death (ATN). In sepsis: efferent arteriolar vasodilation + afferent vasoconstriction + endothelial dysfunction
  • Nephrotoxic ATN: Direct tubular cell toxicity from aminoglycosides (accumulate in proximal tubules), contrast (vasoconstriction + direct toxicity), myoglobin (Heme proteins → free radicals), vancomycin, NSAIDs
  • Inflammatory: Sepsis-induced AKI — cytokine-mediated endothelial dysfunction, microvascular coagulation, mitochondrial dysfunction. NOT just ischaemia — cellular metabolic failure even with normal blood flow
  • Obstructive: Raised intratubular pressure → back-pressure against filtration → ↓ GFR. Rapidly reversible if obstruction relieved within hours

The AKI-Fluid Overload Vicious Cycle

AKI → oliguria → fluid accumulation → pulmonary oedema → ↓ O₂ delivery → worsening AKI → more fluid given → more overload. Fluid overload (>10% body weight gain) independently increases mortality. Break the cycle with appropriate fluid assessment and early RRT for fluid removal if needed.

AKI is defined by functional OR structural criteria (KDIGO 2026 expansion from 2012):

AKD (Acute Kidney Disease) — New Entity in KDIGO 2026 New

AKD is defined as kidney dysfunction (by AKI criteria OR GFR <60 ml/min/1.73m² OR GFR decrease ≥35% OR SCr increase >50% from baseline) with duration ≤3 months. AKD fills the gap between AKI (≤7 days) and CKD (>3 months).

New KDIGO 2026: Use the criterion (SCr or UO) giving the highest stage. B1 (positive damage biomarker) with any creatinine stage indicates structural kidney damage — higher risk of progression.

Furosemide Stress Test (FST) — New KDIGO 2026 Recommendation

KDIGO 2026 — Suggest using FST (2C adults, 2B children)

Indication: Euvolaemic or hypervolaemic patients with Stage 1 or 2 AKI — predict risk of progression to Stage 3.

Protocol: Furosemide 1.0–1.5 mg/kg IV (furosemide-naive) or 1.0 mg/kg (prior furosemide). Measure UO over 2 hours.

Interpretation: UO <200 ml in 2h → high risk of progression to Stage 3 AKI/RRT (sensitivity 87%, specificity 84%). UO ≥200 ml → low risk.

Nephrotoxic Drugs — Stop or Adjust in AKI

Stop These in AKI

NSAIDs | ACE inhibitors / ARBs (unless strongly indicated) | Metformin (lactic acidosis) | Spironolactone (hyperkalaemia) | Aminoglycosides (switch to alternative) | IV contrast (use iso-osmolar; pre-hydrate) | Vancomycin + Piperacillin-Taz combination (↑ nephrotoxicity synergistically)

Prevention of AKI — KDIGO 2026

  • Haemodynamic optimisation: MAP >65 mmHg; avoid hypovolaemia AND excessive fluid overload
  • Buffered crystalloids: Strong recommendation (1B) — balanced crystalloids over 0.9% NaCl for volume expansion (except TBI where 0.9% saline preferred)
  • Avoid nephrotoxins — review all drugs; avoid unnecessary contrast
  • Contrast AKI prevention: IV hydration 0.9% NaCl or sodium bicarbonate 1–1.5 ml/kg/h × 3–12h pre/post procedure; iso-osmolar contrast; minimize contrast volume
  • Glycaemic control: Target 140–180 mg/dL in ICU
  • Elective major abdominal surgery: Liberal fluid strategy (positive balance 1–2 kg at 24h) — Strong recommendation (1B) — reduces AKI risk vs restrictive
  • Vasopressors: Use in conjunction with fluids when haemodynamically unstable — MAP >65 mmHg target

Conservative Management of Established AKI

  • Fluid balance: Avoid both hypovolaemia AND fluid overload; use dynamic assessment (PLR, PPV) to guide fluid decisions
  • Diuretics: Furosemide for volume overload management — does NOT prevent AKI progression or improve renal recovery
  • Nutrition: Normal protein (0.8–1.2 g/kg/day); higher if on CRRT (1.5–2 g/kg/day — amino acid losses through filter)
  • Metabolic acidosis: IV bicarbonate if pH <7.20 without urgent RRT indication — KDIGO 2026 suggests bicarbonate over RRT for isolated acidosis (2C)
  • Potassium: K <5.5 — dietary restriction ± resonium; K 5.5–6.5 → insulin-dextrose + salbutamol; K >6.5 or ECG changes → urgent RRT or calcium gluconate + shifts
  • Drug dose adjustment: All renally-cleared drugs — see drug dosing section

Indications for Renal Replacement Therapy

Absolute Indications — AEIOU Mnemonic
  • Acidosis — metabolic acidosis pH <7.1 refractory to bicarbonate
  • Electrolytes — hyperkalaemia K >6.5 with ECG changes, or K >7.0 regardless
  • Ingestion — dialysable toxins (methanol, ethylene glycol, lithium, salicylates, metformin)
  • Overload — pulmonary oedema refractory to diuretics
  • Uraemia — encephalopathy, pericarditis, uraemic bleeding; urea >150–200 mg/dL

Timing of RRT — KDIGO 2026 Position

KDIGO 2026 — Table 41: Deferred RRT Initiation Strategy

KDIGO 2026 supports a deferred (indication-based) RRT strategy for critically ill adults with AKI. This is consistent with STARRT-AKI (2020), which showed NO benefit from accelerated RRT initiation. Do NOT initiate RRT prophylactically. Wait for an absolute indication or clinical deterioration despite conservative management.

Clinical Pearls

💡 Pearl 1 — Check the Catheter First

Before any AKI workup, confirm the urinary catheter is patent and correctly placed. Kinked, blocked, or malpositioned catheters are the most common cause of iatrogenic oliguria. Flush with 10 ml sterile water.

💡 Pearl 2 — AKD is Now a Distinct Entity (KDIGO 2026)

If kidney dysfunction persists beyond 7 days but less than 90 days, this is now called AKD. These patients need close nephrology follow-up — 30–40% will progress to CKD. Don't discharge without outpatient kidney function monitoring plan.

💡 Pearl 3 — Furosemide Stress Test for Progression Risk

KDIGO 2026 recommends FST: Give furosemide 1–1.5 mg/kg IV to euvolaemic/hypervolaemic Stage 1–2 AKI patients. UO <200 ml in 2h = high risk of Stage 3 AKI → prepare for RRT.

💡 Pearl 4 — Balanced Crystalloids Over Normal Saline (Strong Rec)

KDIGO 2026 Strong (1B): Buffered crystalloids (Ringer's Lactate, PlasmaLyte) over 0.9% NaCl for AKI patients. Hyperchloraemia from NS causes renal vasoconstriction and ↑ AKI risk. Exception: TBI (use 0.9% NaCl).

💡 Pearl 5 — Normal SCr Can Miss AKI

Serum creatinine can be falsely low in AKI: muscle wasting (ICU), low muscle mass (elderly, women, cirrhosis). KDIGO 2026 now includes serum cystatin C as alternative — more accurate in these settings. Use both.

💡 Pearl 6 — Don't Combine Vancomycin + Pip-Taz

Synergistic nephrotoxicity proven — combination increases AKI risk 3-5 fold vs either alone. KDIGO 2026 lists this as a drug combination causing AKI. Use vancomycin + meropenem instead if broad-spectrum coverage needed.

💡 Pearl 7 — Liberal Fluids After Major Abdominal Surgery

KDIGO 2026 Strong (1B): For elective major abdominal surgery, use a LIBERAL intravenous fluid strategy (positive balance 1–2 kg at 24h) over restrictive. This reduces postoperative AKI. Counter-intuitive but strongly supported.

💡 Pearl 8 — India: Tropical AKI

KDIGO 2026 includes a table of tropical infections causing AKI (Table 24). In India: Leptospirosis, Malaria (P. falciparum), Scrub typhus, Dengue, Snake bite (Russell's viper). Always check for tropical causes in appropriate epidemiological context.

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake 1 — Using Vancomycin + Piperacillin-Tazobactam Together

Synergistic nephrotoxicity — 3–5× higher AKI risk. KDIGO 2026 specifically lists this combination. Use vancomycin + meropenem instead when broad coverage is needed.

❌ Mistake 2 — Starting RRT Too Early Without Indication

STARRT-AKI trial: no benefit from accelerated RRT initiation. KDIGO 2026 supports deferred strategy. Do NOT start RRT on creatinine alone — wait for absolute indication (AEIOU). Unnecessary RRT = line complications, hypotension, unnecessary anticoagulation.

❌ Mistake 3 — Using 0.9% Normal Saline for All AKI Patients

KDIGO 2026 Strong (1B): buffered crystalloids over 0.9% NaCl. Hyperchloraemia from normal saline causes renal vasoconstriction, worsens AKI, and causes hyperchloraemic acidosis. Use Ringer's Lactate or PlasmaLyte (except TBI).

❌ Mistake 4 — Morphine in AKI

Morphine-6-glucuronide (M6G) accumulates in AKI → prolonged sedation, respiratory depression. Use FENTANYL instead — no active metabolites, safe in all degrees of renal failure. This is a classic exam and bedside trap.

❌ Mistake 5 — Not Prescribing Higher Effluent Dose

KDIGO recommends ≥20 ml/kg/hr DELIVERED. Due to filter downtime (~25%), you must PRESCRIBE 25 ml/kg/hr to achieve 20 ml/kg/hr net. The RENAL trial showed higher doses (40 ml/kg/hr) don't help, but under-dosing does harm.

Exam Pearls (DrNB / PDCC / IFCCM)

Q: What is AKD and how does it differ from AKI? → New KDIGO 2026 entity. AKD = kidney dysfunction lasting 7 days–3 months. AKI is ≤7 days; CKD is >90 days. AKD is the intermediate/recovery phase with significant risk of progression to CKD.

Q: KDIGO 2026 recommendation on fluid choice in AKI? → Strong (1B): Buffered crystalloids (balanced solutions) over 0.9% NaCl. Exception: TBI patients → 0.9% saline preferred. Colloids (albumin, starches): recommend AGAINST starches (1B); crystalloids alone preferred.

Q: When should you start RRT per KDIGO 2026? → Deferred (indication-based) strategy. Start when AEIOU criteria met: Acidosis pH <7.1, Electrolytes (K >6.5 with ECG changes), Ingestion (dialysable toxins), Overload (refractory pulmonary oedema), Uraemia (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding).

Q: CRRT effluent dose — what to prescribe to deliver 20 ml/kg/hr? → Prescribe 25 ml/kg/hr. Filter downtime (~25%) reduces effective delivery. RENAL trial: >25 ml/kg/hr = no additional benefit.

Q: What is the furosemide stress test and what does it predict? → Furosemide 1–1.5 mg/kg IV to euvolaemic Stage 1–2 AKI. UO <200 ml/2h = high risk of Stage 3 AKI/RRT requirement (sensitivity 87%, specificity 84%). KDIGO 2026 recommends it (2C).

Q: Why is serum cystatin C preferred over SCr in ICU? → SCr is affected by muscle mass, catabolic state, and generation rate. ICU patients are often sarcopenic or hypercatabolic → SCr underestimates AKI severity. Cystatin C produced at constant rate regardless of muscle mass.
