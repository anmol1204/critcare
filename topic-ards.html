Definition & Staging

2023 Global ARDS Definition (updated from Berlin)

The 2023 Global Definition expanded ARDS to include non-intubated patients and resource-limited settings. All criteria must be present within 1 week of a known clinical insult:

  • Bilateral opacities on CXR or CT (not fully explained by effusions, collapse, or nodules)
  • Respiratory failure not fully explained by cardiac failure or fluid overload
  • Oxygenation impairment with SpO2/FiO2 ≤ 315 on O2 ≥ 0.3 (non-intubated) OR P/F ≤ 300 on PEEP ≥ 5 (intubated)

Pathophysiology

ARDS results from diffuse alveolar damage (DAD). Two phases:

  • Exudative (0–7 days): Alveolar flooding with protein-rich fluid, hyaline membranes, alveolar collapse. Diffuse consolidation + atelectasis = "baby lung" (only 20–30% of lung is aerated and recruitable).
  • Proliferative (7–14 days): Type II pneumocyte proliferation, fibroblast infiltration, early fibrosis.

Driving pressure (ΔP = Pplat − PEEP) reflects the stress applied to the aerated baby lung. ΔP > 15 cmH2O is independently associated with mortality.

Ventilator-Induced Lung Injury (VILI)

Overdistension (volutrauma), repetitive opening/closing (atelectrauma), and biotrauma drive secondary lung injury. The whole point of lung-protective ventilation is minimising VILI.

Diagnosis

Common causes to identify and treat:

  • Direct (pulmonary): pneumonia, aspiration, pulmonary contusion, inhalation injury
  • Indirect (extrapulmonary): sepsis (most common), pancreatitis, massive transfusion, DIC, major surgery

Exclude cardiac pulmonary oedema: BNP/NT-proBNP, echocardiography, clinical context. PCWP > 18 mmHg suggests cardiogenic cause (but can coexist).

Lung-Protective Ventilation (ARDSNet)

ARDSNet targets — apply to ALL ventilated ARDS
  • Tidal volume: 6 ml/kg predicted body weight (PBW) — start at 8, reduce to 6
  • Plateau pressure (Pplat): ≤ 30 cmH2O
  • Driving pressure (ΔP = Pplat − PEEP): aim < 14–15 cmH2O
  • Respiratory rate: 14–35/min to maintain pH ≥ 7.30
  • Permissive hypercapnia: acceptable PaCO2 up to 55–60 (pH ≥ 7.25)
  • SpO2 target: 88–95% (FiO2 as low as possible)

Calculating predicted body weight (PBW)

Males: 50 + 2.3 × (height in inches − 60)
Females: 45.5 + 2.3 × (height in inches − 60)

Or use: Males PBW (kg) ≈ height (cm) − 100; Females ≈ height (cm) − 105 (rough estimate)

PEEP Strategy

PEEP prevents alveolar derecruitment and reduces atelectrauma. Higher PEEP improves oxygenation but risks haemodynamic compromise and overdistension.

For moderate-severe ARDS, consider higher PEEP titration guided by driving pressure — reduce PEEP if ΔP increases (recruitable lung is limited). Recruitment manoeuvres should be used cautiously (ART trial showed harm with sustained inflations).

Prone Positioning

PROSEVA trial — most impactful ARDS intervention

Prone positioning ≥ 16 hours/day in severe ARDS (P/F < 150) reduced 28-day mortality from 32.8% to 16%. NNT = 6. This is a strong recommendation.

Indications

  • P/F < 150 on PEEP ≥ 5 cmH2O, FiO2 ≥ 0.6 — after ≥ 12–24h of optimised supine ventilation
  • Consider early: within 36 hours of intubation

Procedure summary

  • Minimum 4 staff members (3 at bedside, 1 managing head/ETT)
  • Secure all lines, pause NGT feeds, give prophylactic sedation
  • Turn to prone in one smooth manoeuvre
  • Head position: swimmer's position, alternate every 2h
  • Target ≥ 16 consecutive hours prone; return supine if SpO2 or haemodynamics worsen significantly

Contraindications

Relative: spinal instability, open abdomen, facial fractures, elevated ICP, pregnancy. Absolute: few — proning is generally safe if done carefully.

Adjunctive Therapies

Neuromuscular blockade (NMB)

Cisatracurium 0.37 mg/kg bolus then 0.03–0.06 mg/kg/hr infusion for 48h in P/F < 150. ACURASYS trial showed benefit; ROSE trial was neutral. Current practice: use selectively for severe ventilator dyssynchrony or refractory hypoxaemia. 2A

High-flow nasal oxygen (HFNO)

For mild-moderate ARDS (P/F 200–300) in non-intubated patients. Flow 40–60 L/min, FiO2 titrated. FLORALI trial showed benefit over conventional O2. Watch for delayed intubation — escalate early if ROX index (SpO2/FiO2 ÷ RR) < 4.88 at 2h.

Inhaled nitric oxide / Prostacyclin

Improve oxygenation transiently (bridge to ECMO or as rescue). No mortality benefit. Use as rescue if P/F < 80 despite prone + optimal vent. 2B

ECMO

Veno-venous (VV) ECMO for reversible severe ARDS (P/F < 80 on optimal therapy). EOLIA trial: ECMO did not meet primary endpoint but had crossover — expert centres use it as rescue. Refer early to ECMO-capable centre.

Fluid Strategy

After initial resuscitation, use a conservative fluid strategy. The FACTT trial showed conservative fluids improved ventilator-free days with no increase in non-pulmonary organ failure.

  • Target neutral to negative fluid balance after haemodynamic stability achieved
  • Daily weights, fluid balance, assess signs of fluid overload
  • Judicious diuresis (furosemide) to achieve neutral balance once haemodynamics allow
