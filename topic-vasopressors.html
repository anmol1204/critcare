Always rule out and treat obstructive and hypovolaemic causes before starting vasopressors. Vasopressors do not replace volume.

Avoid Dopamine

SOAP II trial (2010): Dopamine vs noradrenaline in shock — dopamine caused significantly more arrhythmias and higher mortality in cardiogenic shock. Use noradrenaline as the first-line vasopressor.

Septic Shock Algorithm

Step-up vasopressor approach
  • Step 1: Noradrenaline — titrate to MAP ≥ 65 mmHg
  • Step 2: NE dose > 0.25 mcg/kg/min → add Vasopressin 0.03 U/min (fixed) — spares NE, reduces arrhythmia
  • Step 3: Still refractory → add Adrenaline (replace or add to NE) OR consider Hydrocortisone 200mg/day
  • Step 4: Evidence of low CO (cold extremities, elevated lactate, ScvO2 <65%) → add Dobutamine 5–10 mcg/kg/min
  • Step 5 (rescue): Consider Methylene blue 1–2 mg/kg, Angiotensin II, or ECMO

Target MAP 65–70 mmHg in most patients. Higher MAP (80–85) only for patients with pre-existing hypertension and early AKI who don't tolerate lower MAP (SEPSISPAM trial).

Cardiogenic Shock

Cardiogenic shock: CO ↓, SVR ↑, PCWP ↑. Primary goal is to improve CO and tissue perfusion.

Drug choices

  • Dobutamine 5–10 mcg/kg/min — first line inotrope, ↑CO, ↓SVR
  • If hypotensive + low CO: Noradrenaline + Dobutamine combination
  • Levosimendan — where available; particularly useful in post-AMI and decompensated HF; does not raise O2 demand
  • Avoid pure vasopressors (phenylephrine, vasopressin) alone — worsen afterload

Mechanical support

IABP (intra-aortic balloon pump) — no survival benefit (IABP-SHOCK II trial) but widely used. Impella/VA-ECMO — for severe refractory cardiogenic shock. Early transfer to ECMO centre if available.

Anaphylaxis

Adrenaline is the only first-line drug
  • Adrenaline 0.5 mg IM (1:1000) into anterolateral thigh — give immediately
  • If no response in 5–15 min → repeat IM adrenaline
  • IV adrenaline only in ICU with cardiac monitoring: 50–100 mcg boluses or 0.05–0.5 mcg/kg/min infusion
  • Adjuncts (secondary): Hydrocortisone 200mg IV, Chlorpheniramine 10mg IV, Salbutamol for bronchospasm
  • Position supine with legs elevated; IV fluids 1–2L rapidly

Clinical Pearls

  • Always use an arterial line when titrating vasopressors — NIBP unreliable in shock states
  • Central line preferred for sustained vasopressor infusions; peripheral acceptable for short periods with frequent site checks
  • Lactate is the best marker of tissue perfusion — target clearance, not just MAP
  • ScvO2 < 65% suggests mixed problem (low CO component) — consider dobutamine
  • Noradrenaline infusions: 4–8 mg in 50ml (80–160 mcg/ml); adjust concentration to clinical situation
  • Monitor for peripheral ischaemia (digits, nose) at high doses of vasoconstrictors
