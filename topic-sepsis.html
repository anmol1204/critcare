Definitions (Sepsis-3, 2016)

Sepsis is life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. It is identified by an acute change in total SOFA score ≥ 2 points attributable to infection.

Septic shock is a subset of sepsis with circulatory and cellular/metabolic dysfunction — defined as vasopressor requirement to maintain MAP ≥ 65 mmHg AND serum lactate > 2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation. Hospital mortality exceeds 40%.

Key Point — Sepsis-3 replaced SIRS

The 2016 Sepsis-3 consensus abandoned the SIRS-based definitions. SIRS is nonspecific. Organ dysfunction (SOFA ≥2) is now the hallmark. qSOFA (RR ≥22, altered mentation, SBP ≤100) is a bedside screening tool, not a diagnostic criterion.

Pathophysiology

Dysregulated immune response

Pathogen-associated molecular patterns (PAMPs) and damage-associated molecular patterns (DAMPs) activate pattern-recognition receptors (TLRs, NLRs), triggering cytokine storm — TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6. This leads to systemic endothelial activation, coagulopathy, and microvascular dysfunction.

Haemodynamic pattern

  • Early (warm shock): High CO, low SVR, warm peripheries, bounding pulse, distributive physiology
  • Late (cold shock): Myocardial depression, fall in CO, peripheral vasoconstriction
  • Microcirculatory failure leads to cellular hypoxia despite normal/high macrocirculatory flow

Organ dysfunction cascade

Renal: afferent arteriolar vasodilation → reduced GFR. Lungs: ARDS in ~40% of septic shock. Liver: cholestasis, coagulopathy. Brain: septic encephalopathy. Adrenal: relative adrenal insufficiency in refractory shock.

A SOFA score ≥ 2 from baseline in a patient with suspected infection = Sepsis.

qSOFA (quick bedside screen)

Score 1 point each for: RR ≥ 22, altered mentation (GCS <15), SBP ≤ 100. qSOFA ≥ 2 → suspect sepsis, reassess with full SOFA. Not for diagnosis, useful for triage.

Hour-1 Bundle (SSC 2024)

Do all 5 within 1 hour of recognising sepsis
  • 1. Measure lactate — re-measure at 2h if initial lactate ≥ 2 mmol/L
  • 2. Blood cultures — at least 2 sets before antibiotics (do not delay antibiotics)
  • 3. Broad-spectrum antibiotics — within 1 hour for septic shock; within 3h for sepsis without shock
  • 4. 30 ml/kg crystalloid IV — if hypotensive (SBP<90 or MAP<65) or lactate ≥ 4 mmol/L
  • 5. Vasopressors — if MAP remains < 65 mmHg during or after fluid resuscitation
Fluid caution in India

The 30ml/kg fluid bolus is for resource-rich settings. In patients with AKI, heart failure, or resource-limited ICUs, reassess fluid responsiveness with dynamic parameters (PLR, PPV, IVC collapse) before each 250–500ml bolus. Avoid indiscriminate fluid loading.

Haemodynamic Management

Fluid resuscitation targets

  • MAP ≥ 65 mmHg (target 65–70 in most; higher 80–85 only in known hypertension with AKI — SEPSISPAM trial)
  • Lactate clearance ≥ 10% per 2 hours
  • UO > 0.5 ml/kg/hr
  • Use dynamic fluid responsiveness parameters — passive leg raise (PLR), PPV, IVC collapsibility

De-escalation

  • Review culture results at 48–72 hours — narrow spectrum based on sensitivities
  • Procalcitonin-guided de-escalation reduces antibiotic duration (SAPS trial)
  • Duration: 7 days is sufficient for most infections; shorter acceptable if good clinical response

Source control

Identify and control the source as early as possible — drain abscess, remove infected device, debride necrotic tissue. Delay in source control is independently associated with mortality.

Adjunctive Therapies

Corticosteroids

Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day (50mg IV q6h or 200mg/24h infusion) — give if vasopressor refractory shock despite adequate volume and escalating vasopressors. 2A

Do not use ACTH stimulation test to guide steroid use. Wean hydrocortisone when vasopressors are being weaned (APROCCHSS, ADRENAL trials).

Glycaemic control

Target blood glucose 140–180 mg/dL using insulin infusion. Avoid hypoglycaemia (<80 mg/dL) which is independently associated with mortality. Tight control (80–110) shown to cause harm (NICE-SUGAR trial). 1A

Blood transfusion

Restrictive threshold: transfuse RBC when Hb < 7 g/dL (70 g/L) in adults without active ischaemia/bleeding. Higher threshold (Hb <9) only for acute MI, unstable angina, or severe hypoxaemia. (TRISS trial) 1B

DVT prophylaxis

LMWH preferred over UFH. Mechanical (compression stockings) if anticoagulation contraindicated.

Stress ulcer prophylaxis

Only in patients with ≥1 risk factor (mechanical ventilation >48h, coagulopathy, prior GI bleed, steroid use). Pantoprazole 40mg IV OD.

Monitoring & Targets

  • MAP ≥ 65 mmHg (continuously on arterial line ideally)
  • Lactate — trending to normal (<2 mmol/L); clearance ≥ 10% per 2h
  • UO > 0.5 ml/kg/hr (hourly)
  • SOFA score — daily trending
  • Glucose 140–180 mg/dL
  • Blood cultures — 48–72h results for de-escalation
  • Procalcitonin — baseline + day 3, 5 for de-escalation guidance
  • Echo — assess cardiac function if refractory shock
