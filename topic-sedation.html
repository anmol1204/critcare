ABCDEF Bundle (ICU Liberation)

Full bundle compliance is associated with reduced mortality, mechanical ventilation days, delirium, and physical/cognitive impairment at 1 year (multicenter cohort, Ely 2019).

Analgesia-First Approach (eCASH)

eCASH: early Comfort using Analgesia, minimal Sedatives, maximal Humane care (PADIS 2018). The modern paradigm: treat pain before adding sedation. Most ICU agitation is pain-driven.

eCASH Principles
  • Start with IV opioid analgesia (fentanyl infusion preferred) — titrate to CPOT 0–2
  • Only add sedation if agitation persists after adequate analgesia
  • Use lightest effective sedation: RASS −1 to 0 for most ventilated patients
  • Perform daily SAT (spontaneous awakening trial) — pause all sedation, reassess
  • Never sedate without first treating pain

Spontaneous Awakening Trial (SAT) Protocol

  1. Safety screen: no active seizures, no alcohol/benzo withdrawal, no agitation (RASS >+2), no active paralysis, no ↑ ICP, no FiO₂ >0.7 or PEEP >12
  2. Stop all sedatives (continue analgesics)
  3. Monitor for 4h: if opens eyes to voice, follows commands → PASS → proceed to SBT
  4. FAIL criteria (restart sedation at 50%): agitation (RASS >+2), sustained SpO₂ <88%, respiratory distress, new arrhythmia

Propofol Infusion Syndrome (PRIS) — Life-Threatening

Features: Metabolic acidosis + ↑ lactate + rhabdomyolysis + ↑ CK + cardiac failure + renal failure + ↑ triglycerides. Risk factors: Dose >4 mg/kg/hr, duration >48h, high-carbohydrate feeds, catecholamines, steroids, critical illness. Action: Stop propofol immediately; switch to alternative. Monitor TG, CK, lactate, pH daily on propofol infusions.

ICU Delirium

Delirium affects 60–80% of mechanically ventilated patients. Associated with higher mortality, prolonged MV, longer ICU/hospital stay, and long-term cognitive impairment.

CAM-ICU Assessment (perform q8–12h)

Positive (= delirium) if Feature 1 AND Feature 2 AND (Feature 3 OR Feature 4):

1. Acute change or fluctuationHistory from nurse/family; chart reviewAcute change from baseline OR fluctuating mental status in last 24h

Non-Pharmacological Prevention (First Line — All Patients)

  • Daily reorientation: name, place, date; visible clock and calendar
  • Sleep hygiene: Cluster care at night; reduce alarms; ear plugs; eye masks; dim lights 10pm–6am
  • Early mobilisation: passive → active exercises from day 1
  • Hearing aids, glasses — ensure patients have access to their aids
  • Minimise anticholinergic medications, benzodiazepines
  • Treat reversible precipitants: pain, urinary retention, constipation, metabolic disturbances
  • Normalise sleep-wake cycle: avoid daytime sedation, minimise nighttime interventions

Pharmacological Management of Delirium

No Drug has Mortality Benefit for ICU Delirium (MIND-USA, HOPE-ICU)

Neither haloperidol nor ziprasidone reduced mortality or ventilator-free days in ICU delirium (MIND-USA 2018). Antipsychotics are used for patient safety and comfort — not as first-line prevention. Non-pharmacological strategies are mandatory and should be tried first.

Special Situations

ARDS / High Ventilatory Support

Deeper sedation (RASS −2 to −3) for ventilator synchrony and proning. Use propofol + fentanyl. Add cisatracurium if severe dyssynchrony (NMB). Resume lighter sedation as soon as ventilator support decreases.

Status Epilepticus

Propofol infusion or midazolam infusion as anaesthetic agents; titrate to EEG burst suppression. Continuous EEG monitoring mandatory.

Liver Failure

Avoid benzodiazepines (precipitate/worsen hepatic encephalopathy). Use fentanyl + low-dose dexmedetomidine. Propofol in low doses if haemodynamically stable.

Alcohol/Benzodiazepine Withdrawal

Benzodiazepines are first-line (diazepam 5–10 mg q1h PRN, or chlordiazepoxide 25–100 mg q6h). CIWA-Ar protocol for symptom-triggered therapy. IV thiamine 100–500 mg before any glucose. Propofol or phenobarbital 10–15 mg/kg IV over 1h for refractory withdrawal.

Neurological ICU (Raised ICP)

Avoid ketamine (raises ICP — relative contraindication). Propofol + fentanyl preferred. Target RASS −2 to −3 acutely; titrate ICP-directed. Maintain CPP ≥60 mmHg throughout.
