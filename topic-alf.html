Definition

Acute Liver Failure (ALF) = severe acute liver injury with coagulopathy (INR ≥ 1.5) AND encephalopathy in a patient without pre-existing liver disease, within 26 weeks of onset of jaundice.

  • Hyperacute: Encephalopathy within 7 days of jaundice (paracetamol, HAV, HEV) — better prognosis
  • Acute: 8–28 days
  • Subacute: 4–26 weeks — worst prognosis without transplant

ICU Management

General

  • ICU admission for Grade II encephalopathy or above
  • Intubate for Grade III–IV (airway protection, ICP management)
  • Treat precipitants: infection, GI bleed, constipation, drugs
  • Low-protein diet is outdated — provide normal protein (1–1.2g/kg/day)
  • Lactulose: 30–60ml TDS titrated to 2–3 soft stools/day; rifaximin 550mg BD

Coagulopathy

Do NOT correct coagulopathy prophylactically

INR in ALF is a prognostic marker. Do not give FFP or vitamin K unless active bleeding or invasive procedure planned. Liver has balanced deficiency of pro- and anti-coagulant factors — INR overestimates bleeding risk. Use TEG/ROTEM to guide transfusion.

Cerebral oedema (Grade III–IV)

  • Head of bed 30°; minimise stimulation
  • Hypertonic saline (target Na 145–150 mEq/L) or Mannitol 0.5–1g/kg IV for ICP spikes
  • Avoid hypoxia, hypercapnia, hypoglycaemia, fever
  • ICP monitoring controversial — consider in Grade IV with worsening
  • Target CPP > 50–60 mmHg

Renal

AKI in 50% — avoid nephrotoxins; CRRT preferred over IHD (haemodynamic instability)

Infection

Empirical broad-spectrum antibiotics if suspected (fever, worsening encephalopathy) — cultures first. Antifungals (fluconazole) for Grade III–IV.

Glucose

Hypoglycaemia common (glycogen stores depleted) — 10–20% dextrose infusion; check glucose q1–2h.

Liver Transplant Criteria

King's College Criteria

List for transplant if ANY criterion met

Paracetamol-induced:
Arterial pH < 7.30 (after resuscitation) — OR — all 3: PT >100s (INR>6.5) + Creatinine >300 µmol/L + Grade III–IV encephalopathy

Non-paracetamol:
PT >100s alone — OR — any 3 of 5: age <10 or >40; aetiology (non-A non-B, DILI, Wilson's); jaundice-to-encephalopathy >7 days; PT >50s; bilirubin >300 µmol/L

Refer to transplant centre early — outcome dramatically better with living-related liver transplant in India.
