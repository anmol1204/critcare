📘 1 · Washington Manual of Critical Care, 4th Ed

Washington Manual Summary

"For COPD patients requiring ICU admission, one of every four will not survive the hospitalisation, and 40% of survivors will be readmitted within a year. NIV is the cornerstone of management for acute hypercapnic exacerbations and has Level A evidence for reducing intubation and mortality."

Washington Manual of Critical Care, 4th Ed. Kollef & Witt (eds). Wolters Kluwer 2023. [Paraphrasing Marino Ch.23, p.405]

📗 2 · Marino's The ICU Book, 5th Edition (2025)

Marino Physiology — COPD in the ICU

"In cases of severe COPD with chronic hypercapnia, high concentrations of inhaled O₂ can promote further increases in arterial PCO₂. This was originally attributed to loss of hypoxic ventilatory drive, but more recent studies have shown that the oxygen-induced rise in arterial PCO₂ is not accompanied by a decrease in ventilatory drive. Oxygen unloading of CO₂ from haemoglobin (the Haldane Effect) may play a role."

Marino PL. The ICU Book, 5th Ed. Chapter 23: Asthma and COPD in the ICU, p.416–417. Wolters Kluwer; 2025.

Step 1 — O₂ in COPD (Marino Ch.23, p.416–417)

📗 Marino Ch.23, p.416 — Why High O₂ is Dangerous in COPD

High-concentration O₂ → ↑ arterial PCO₂ via two mechanisms:

  • Haldane Effect: O₂ displaces CO₂ from haemoglobin → CO₂ released into plasma → ↑ PaCO₂
  • V/Q mismatch: High O₂ abolishes hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction → blood flow to poorly ventilated areas ↑ → ↑ dead space → ↑ CO₂ retention

Marino notes: "It is important to avoid high concentrations of inhaled O₂ in COPD patients with chronic hypercapnia." Target SpO₂ 88–92%. Use Venturi mask (24%, 28%, 31%) for precise FiO₂ delivery.

Step 2 — Antibiotics in COPD (Marino Ch.23, p.416)

📗 Marino Ch.23, p.416 — Antibiotic Indications

Marino (p.416): "Only about 50% [of COPD exacerbations] are caused by treatable bacterial infections, and this limits the benefit of antimicrobial therapy." Indications for antibiotics:

  • Increase in sputum production
  • Grossly purulent sputum
  • Need for noninvasive or invasive mechanical ventilation

Marino: "It is reasonable to assume that all ICU admissions for acute exacerbations of COPD are candidates for antibiotic therapy." Common isolates: H. influenzae, S. pneumoniae; Pseudomonas in ventilator-dependent patients. Empiric: cefepime covers both. Duration: 5–7 days.

Step 3 — Ventilating the COPD Patient (Marino Ch.23, p.418)

📗 Marino Ch.23, p.418–419 — Ventilator Strategy

Dynamic hyperinflation (auto-PEEP) is the dominant hazard in ventilated COPD. Marino's ventilator adjustments to limit dynamic hyperinflation:

  • Avoid inflation volumes exceeding 6–8 ml/kg IBW
  • Maximise expiratory time: slow RR with sedation if possible AND increase inspiratory flow rate so inspiration occupies only 1/3 of respiratory cycle
  • Monitor intrinsic PEEP with expiratory hold manoeuvre

Critical insight: In ventilated COPD patients, target their chronic baseline CO₂ (e.g., 55–65 mmHg), NOT 40 mmHg. Normalising CO₂ → alkalosis → renal HCO₃ excretion → on extubation, CO₂ rises with no buffer → failed weaning.

📋 3 · GOLD 2026 Guidelines

GOLD 2026 — Key Changes

Global Initiative for COPD (GOLD). Global Strategy for Diagnosis, Management, and Prevention of COPD: 2026 Report. Published December 2025. goldcopd.org
GOLD 2026 — Exacerbation Threshold Lowered Major Change
ONE moderate exacerbation now triggers treatment escalation to Group E (previously needed ≥2 moderate or ≥1 severe). Goal: achieve "low disease activity state" = no exacerbations. Earlier escalation to triple therapy (LAMA+LABA+ICS) if eosinophils ≥300.
GOLD 2026 — Chapter 4 (Exacerbations) Completely Revised Major Revision
Exacerbations defined by: worsening of symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variation requiring change in medication. Severity: Mild (SABA only) | Moderate (SABA + steroids ± antibiotics) | Severe (hospitalisation). Chapter 4 has new figures and management algorithms.
GOLD 2026 — Biologic Therapy (NEW)New 2026
New Figure 3.11 on biologic therapy in COPD: dupilumab, itepekimab (anti-IL-33) for COPD with type 2 inflammation (eosinophils ≥300). Reduces exacerbation risk in selected patients.
GOLD 2026 — Vaccinations Updated Updated
RSV vaccine added (respiratory syncytial virus — approved for adults ≥60 years); updated influenza and COVID-19 recommendations. Pneumococcal vaccine (PCV20 or PCV15+PPSV23) recommended for all COPD patients.
NIV in Acute Exacerbation Strong · Evidence A
NIV (BiPAP) for pH <7.35 + PaCO₂ >45 mmHg: reduces intubation rate, mortality, and ICU LOS. IPAP 10–16, EPAP 4–6 cmH₂O. Check ABG at 1–2h. ROC criteria (Ferrer): success predictors include improved pH and PaCO₂ at 1h. Failure = intubate without delay.

🗺 5 · Clinical Flowchart

COPD Exacerbation — Step by Step

1

Assess Severity

pH, PaCO₂, SpO₂, RR, consciousness. DECAF score for mortality risk. pH <7.35 + PaCO₂ >45 = NIV candidate. pH <7.25 = consider immediate NIV/intubation depending on clinical status.

2

Controlled O₂ (NOT High-Flow)

Venturi mask 24% or 28% (preferred — precise FiO₂). Target SpO₂ 88–92%. Marino (p.416): high O₂ → Haldane Effect → ↑ PaCO₂. Check ABG 30–60 min after starting O₂ — detect CO₂ rise. Never withhold O₂ entirely from hypoxic patient.

3

Bronchodilators + Steroids + Antibiotics

Salbutamol neb q4–6h + Ipratropium q6h. Prednisolone 40 mg OD × 5 days (GOLD 2026). Antibiotics if: purulent sputum, increased sputum, or mechanical ventilation (Marino: all ICU admissions are candidates).

4

NIV (pH <7.35 + PaCO₂ >45)

BiPAP: IPAP 10 → titrate to 20–25; EPAP 4–6. FiO₂ to SpO₂ 88–92%. Check ABG at 1–2h. Success: pH improving, PaCO₂ falling, RR <25, SpO₂ target reached. Failure: pH worsening → intubate without delay.

5

Intubation (NIV Failure or Contraindicated)

NIV failure criteria: pH worsening after 1–2h, altered consciousness, unable to tolerate mask, copious secretions, haemodynamic instability. Ventilate: target chronic CO₂ baseline (NOT 40 mmHg), RR 10–14, I:E 1:3–1:4. Monitor auto-PEEP.

6

Weaning — Switch to NIV Early

Attempt NIV-facilitated weaning: extubate early to NIV rather than waiting for full extubation criteria in COPD. Reduces re-intubation and ICU LOS. Target chronic baseline CO₂ during MV — NOT 40 mmHg (will cause post-extubation failure).

⚠️ 6 · Common Mistakes

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake 1 — High-Concentration O₂ in COPD

Marino (Ch.23, p.416): high O₂ → Haldane Effect → CO₂ retention. Target SpO₂ 88–92%. Use Venturi mask (precise FiO₂: 24%, 28%). Check ABG 30–60 min after any O₂ change. Withholding O₂ entirely from a hypoxic COPD patient is also wrong.

❌ Mistake 2 — Targeting PaCO₂ 40 mmHg on Ventilator

Chronic hypercapnic COPD patient: normal PaCO₂ may be 55–65 mmHg with compensatory HCO₃. Ventilating to 40 mmHg = alkalosis → renal HCO₃ excretion → extubation → CO₂ rises with no buffer = failed weaning and re-intubation. Target patient's CHRONIC CO₂ baseline.

❌ Mistake 3 — Not Starting NIV Early Enough

NIV has Evidence A in COPD exacerbation (Cochrane 2017): reduces intubation rate, mortality, ICU LOS. Start NIV when pH <7.35 + PaCO₂ >45. Do not delay for "one more hour of medical therapy." Early NIV prevents progression to intubation.

❌ Mistake 4 — Prolonged Steroid Courses

GOLD 2026: prednisolone 40 mg × 5 days = equivalent to 14-day courses (REDUCE trial). Longer courses = more hyperglycaemia, more muscle weakness, no additional benefit. 5 days is the standard.

❌ Mistake 5 — Antibiotics for Every COPD Exacerbation

Marino (Ch.23, p.416): only ~50% of COPD exacerbations are from treatable bacterial infections. Give antibiotics only if: purulent sputum, ↑ sputum, or mechanical ventilation. Avoid antibiotics for mild exacerbations with clear sputum = drives AMR.

🎓 7 · Exam Pearls — DrNB / PDCC / IFCCM

Exam Pearls

Q: GOLD 2026 major change — exacerbation threshold?
ONE moderate exacerbation now classifies patient as Group E and triggers escalation to triple therapy (LAMA+LABA+ICS). Previously required ≥2 moderate or ≥1 severe. Goal: achieve "low disease activity state" with no exacerbations.

Q: Why is high O₂ dangerous in COPD? (Marino insight)
Marino (Ch.23, p.416): Two mechanisms: (1) Haldane Effect — O₂ displaces CO₂ from Hb → CO₂ released to plasma → ↑ PaCO₂; (2) V/Q mismatch — high O₂ abolishes hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction → blood to poorly ventilated areas → ↑ dead space → ↑ CO₂. Target SpO₂ 88–92% with Venturi mask.

Q: NIV settings and evidence in COPD exacerbation?
BiPAP: IPAP 10–25 cmH₂O, EPAP 4–6 cmH₂O, FiO₂ to SpO₂ 88–92%. Evidence A (Cochrane 2017): reduces intubation rate, mortality, ICU LOS vs medical therapy alone. Check ABG at 1–2h. If pH not improving → intubate without further delay.

Q: Why NOT target PaCO₂ 40 mmHg in ventilated COPD?
Chronic COPD patient has compensatory metabolic alkalosis (↑ HCO₃) for their chronic hypercapnia. Ventilating to PaCO₂ 40 = uncompensated alkalosis → kidneys excrete HCO₃ → extubation → CO₂ rises with no buffer → failed weaning. Target patient's CHRONIC CO₂ baseline (often 55–65 mmHg).

Q: Antibiotic indications in COPD exacerbation (Marino + GOLD)?
Marino (p.416): only if purulent sputum, ↑ sputum production, or need for mechanical ventilation. GOLD 2026: antibiotics also if DECAF score ≥3. All ICU admissions are candidates (Marino). Empirical: amoxicillin-clavulanate; Pseudomonas risk → cefepime or pip-taz. Duration 5–7 days.
